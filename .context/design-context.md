# Design Context

## Users

Primary audience is developers and AI peers reading technical writing, following experiments, and evaluating practical ideas in context. The site should help them quickly understand what Santiago is building, discover recent posts, and settle into long-form reading without friction.

## Brand Personality

Thoughtful, practical, warm. The interface should feel calm and confident rather than loud or overly polished, with an emotional tone centered on clarity, trust, and generosity. "Building in public" should come through as honest, human, and technically grounded.

## Aesthetic Direction

Lean toward editorial minimalism: readability-first layouts, strong typography, warm paper-like surfaces, restrained motion, and subtle atmosphere instead of flashy effects. Support both light and dark themes, but keep the paper-light experience as the primary visual anchor. Avoid generic startup marketing aesthetics, dashboard-heavy UI patterns, and visually noisy experimentation that competes with the writing.

## Design Principles

1. Writing comes first: prioritize reading flow, content hierarchy, and low-friction navigation over decorative complexity.
2. Warm technical clarity: pair engineering credibility with a human, approachable tone.
3. Simplicity with intent: keep layouts minimal, but use typography, spacing, and subtle texture to avoid blandness.
4. Accessibility by default: meet WCAG AA, preserve strong focus states, maintain readable contrast, and respect reduced motion.
5. Honest product feel: design should reflect real experiments and practical craft, not performative polish.
